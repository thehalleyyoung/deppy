Sheaf semantics

The site/presheaf/sheaf/cohomology story behind deppy's notion of bugs and drift.

On this page

Sheaf theory is the mature mathematics of local-to-global problems. Programs are full of local facts; deppy asks when those facts glue into a coherent global story.

Programs as sites

A program induces a site: functions, blocks, APIs, modules, tests, and interfaces become objects; control-flow, dataflow, and inclusion relationships become morphisms.

\[\mathrm{Ty} : \mathrm{Site}(P)^{op} \to \mathrm{TypeLattice}.\]

The sheaf condition

Local sections that agree on overlaps should glue uniquely. If they do not, the failure is informative.

\[s_i|_{U_i \cap U_j} = s_j|_{U_i \cap U_j} \;\forall i,j \Longrightarrow \exists!\, s \in \Gamma(U, \mathrm{Ty}).\]

Why H^1 matters

The first Cech cohomology group measures obstructions to gluing.

\[H^1(U, \mathrm{Ty}) \neq 0 \Longrightarrow \text{there exists a genuine local-to-global inconsistency}.\]
