Type expansion

How a natural-language specification is refined into a typed code-generation plan.

On this page

Planning is the process of expanding a type until its leaves are atomic and its overlaps glue.

\[\Gamma \vdash T \rightsquigarrow_r T^\prime \quad \text{where } r \in \{\Sigma\text{-expand}, \Pi\text{-expand}, \mathrm{Refine}, \mathrm{Identify}, \mathrm{Universe}\}.\]

Sigma expansion

Break one large specification into local components that can each be generated and verified.

Pi expansion

Refine the function-level contracts and dependent interfaces that connect the components.

Identity expansion

Discover and enforce the gluing conditions on overlaps.

Completion criterion

\[\dim H^1(U_{current}, \mathrm{HybridTy}) = 0 \quad \text{and all leaves are atomic}.\]

That criterion turns planning into a typed, measurable process rather than a rhetorical one.
