Controlled oracles

The oracle monad, trust propagation, and the split between decidable and semantic claims.

On this page

deppy treats the LLM as a controlled oracle: powerful, useful, and bounded.

\[T_O(A) = A \times \mathrm{TrustLevel} \times \mathrm{Confidence} \times \mathrm{Provenance}.\]

Weakest-link composition

If one stage is only LLM-judged and another is Z3-proven, the composition should not pretend to be Lean-verified.

\[\mathrm{trust}(m \mathbin{>>=} f) = \min(\mathrm{trust}(m), \mathrm{trust}(f)).\]

Decidability stratification

Natural-language specifications mix decidable and non-decidable content.

\[\varphi = \varphi_d \land \varphi_s, \qquad \varphi_d \in \mathrm{SMT},\; \varphi_s \in \mathrm{Oracle/semantic}.\]
