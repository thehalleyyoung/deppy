Ideation as typing

Cross-domain software ideation as a typed gluing problem over domain sites.

On this page

deppy treats interdisciplinary ideation as a typing problem. Domains are sites, analogies are functors, and shallow analogy is a failure of topology preservation.

\[\mathrm{AnalogyType}(A, B) = \Sigma(F : \mathrm{ConceptMapping}(A,B)).\, \Sigma(\_ : \mathrm{Functor}(F)).\, \Sigma(\_ : \mathrm{TopologyPreserving}(F)).\, \Sigma(v : \mathrm{Value}(F)).\, \top\]

What counts as a deep analogy?

A deep analogy preserves morphisms and covering structure.

Theorem sketch

theorem anti_hallucination_for_ideas
    (candidate : GoodSoftwareIdea S)
    (T : IdeaHybridType)
    (h_intent : T.intentLayer)
    (h_struct : T.structuralLayer)
    (h_sem : T.semanticLayer)
    (h_proof : T.proofLayer)
    (h_impl : T.implementationLayer) :
    not is_idea_hallucination candidate T := by
  unfold is_idea_hallucination ideaInhabits
  simp [h_intent, h_struct, h_sem, h_proof, h_impl]

If an idea is inhabited all the way down its intent, structure, semantics, proof, and implementation layers, it is not just a slogan.

\[H^1(\mathrm{IdeaCover}, \mathrm{IdeaHybridTy}) = 0 \Longleftrightarrow \text{the interdisciplinary story actually glues}.\]
